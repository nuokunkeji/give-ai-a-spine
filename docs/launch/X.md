# X launch thread draft

## Post 1

AI should not agree with your most important assumption just because you stated it confidently.

I open-sourced Spine: a cross-agent reality-check skill for Codex, Claude Code, Cursor, Gemini CLI, and OpenCode.

Give your AI a spine. Less agreement, more honest work. 🧵

## Post 2

Spine does five things on consequential decisions:

1. Clarifies the real goal
2. Separates facts, inference, values, and symbols
3. Checks both your bias and the AI's flattery
4. Finds counterevidence and the weakest assumption
5. Produces one observable next test

## Post 3

The opposite failure matters too.

Spine does not argue for sport, diagnose emotions, mock symbolic language, or turn “17 × 24?” into a life audit. Simple requests stay simple.
